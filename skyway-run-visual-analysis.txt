SKYWAY.RUN VISUAL ANALYSIS
==========================

MAP TECHNOLOGY:
- Uses MapLibre GL JS (open-source Mapbox GL fork) with vector tiles
- Canvas-based rendering (WebGL)
- Custom map style with light gray streets/buildings background
- Vector tile source: self-hosted at skyway.run/api/tile.json

MAP STYLE:
- Background: Light gray (#f0f0f0 or similar)
- Streets: Slightly darker gray lines
- Buildings: Very light gray outlines
- The skyway paths are the PRIMARY visual element - everything else is muted

SKYWAY PATH RENDERING:
- Paths are rendered as thick colored lines (about 6-8px wide)
- Each building/section has a DISTINCT COLOR:
  - Red: Target Center / west side area
  - Green: Gaviidae Common / City Center area  
  - Blue: IDS Center / US Bank Plaza area
  - Purple: Government Center / east side
  - Orange/Brown: Marquette Plaza / north area
  - Dark gray: Parking ramps and peripheral buildings
  - Cyan: Foshay Tower area
  - Magenta/Pink: Target Plaza / south area
- The colors seem to correspond to building OWNERS or ZONES

PATH STYLE:
- Thick lines with rounded ends
- "X" marks appear on paths that are CLOSED or restricted
- Dots/circles at intersections and building entrances
- Building names in ALL CAPS, black text, positioned near buildings

BUILDING LABELS:
- All caps text
- Black color
- Positioned adjacent to the building
- Clear readable font

KEY DIFFERENCES FROM OUR CURRENT MAP:
1. We use a dark background SVG - they use a light map tile background
2. We have thin lines - they have thick colored lines
3. We don't color-code by zone - they do
4. We don't show the street grid - they show it as background context
5. Our labels overlap - theirs are well-positioned
6. They show open/closed status with X marks

APPROACH TO REPLICATE:
- Use react-native-maps on native (Apple Maps) with polyline overlays
- Use Leaflet or MapLibre on web with GeoJSON overlay
- Color-code paths by building zone
- Use thick lines with rounded caps
- Show building labels in all caps
